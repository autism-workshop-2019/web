AUTISM NEUROIMAGING + CONNECTOMICS WORKSHOP, MONTREAL 2019



ABOUT

The “Neuroimaging and connectomics of Autism” workshop will consist of lectures and tutorials from outstanding international and local researchers. The aims are to connect researchers working in varied areas of neuroimaging, and to engage with and build knowledge of neuroimaging in the wider autism research community.
The programme is directed towards scientists and clinicians interested in how recent innovations in neuroimaging can benefit our understanding, diagnosis, and treatment of autism. We welcome individuals with and without neuroimaging experience.

The morning session will consist of a series of short lectures by world-class autism researchers, hosted at the Montreal Neurological Institute. The session topics will include cutting-edge trends and best-pracktice in structural MRI, functional MRI, together with human-animal neuroimaging translation and imaging genomics.

In the afternoon, participants can join a hands-on educational session and BrainHack at the Notman House, where a team of experts will provide tutorials on their cutting-edge imaging analyses. The afternoon session will be limited to 70 participants to ensure an intimate and helpful environment.
