ABOUT

The “Neuroimaging and connectomics of Autism” workshop consist of lectures and tutorials from outstanding international and local researchers. The aims are to connect researchers working in various areas of neuroimaging, and to engage with the wider autism research community.

The programme is directed towards scientists and clinicians interested in how recent innovations in neuroimaging can benefit our understanding, diagnosis, and treatment of autism. We welcome individuals with and without neuroimaging experience.

The morning session will consist of short lectures given by a diverse panel of world-class and emerging autism neuroimaging researchers, hosted at the Montreal Neurological Institute.
Topics range from cutting-edge applications of structural MRI, functional MRI, connectome models, together with human-animal neuroimaging translation and imaging genomics.

In the afternoon, participants can join a hands-on educational session and BrainHack at the Notman House, where a team of experts will provide tutorials on their cutting-edge imaging analyses.
